The Nations Identity

On the topic of culture,  Nepal has been a country that has been influenced heavily by many beliefs due to the vast amount of different backgrounds that reside in the country.  The most common religions in Nepal are Buddhism and Hinduism,  which have coexisted in the country for hundreds of years.  For a general overview of the geogrpahy,  Nepal is split into 3 main regions,  the Himalaya(to the north),  Hills(middle region) and terai(to the south).  All these regions combine to be home to around 101 ethnic groups in Nepal and over 92 languages spoken in the country.

There are various festivals celebrated by Nepalese people, we will be talking about the main ones:

Dashain

















Also known as the “festival of kites”,  Dashain is the most anticipated and longest celebration of Nepal.  Lasting for a total of 2 weeks,  it is a celebration of Goddess Durga.  It is a time for exchange of gifts and family reunions and worship.

Another aspect of Dashain is flying kites,  which is said to bring good fortune and prosperity to families during this time.

Tihar
















The second biggest festival in Nepal is Tihar and is celebrated 2 weeks after Dashian. Tihar, also referred to as “Diwali” in other parts of South Asia, is the “festival of light”.

In the 5 days of this festiva animals such as Cows, Crows and Dogs are also worshipped. People also celebrate during this time using fireworks and decorating their houses with bright lights to attract Goddess Laxmi(the god of wealth) into their homes.

Rato Machindranath Jatra


















The Rato Machindranath jatra is a chariot festival mainly celebrated by Buddhists in Lalitpur, Nepal. This celebration was organized to celebrate the end of a drought around 640 AD that had devastated the nation.

The chariot is pulled by civilians throughout the city and is taken to a temple in the nearby village of Bungamati. It is a sight to see and a must see if you visit Kathmandu around July depending on the lunar calendar.


Now, we will be looking at some architecture of Nepal


Boudhanath Stupa

Boudhanath Stupa is an historic stupa located in Kathmandu. The stupa is believed to have been built around
the 14th century.

It has a massive semicircle dome, with the towering spire that has eyes of all 4 sides to represent the all-
knowing nature of the Buddha. The overall shape also represents a Buddhist mandala, and expresses the path
towards enlightenment.

Historically, its location is an ancient trade route that spans from Tibet to Kathmandu. The area is also home
to a majority of Tibetan who settled in that area for refuge after China’s invasion of Tibet.

The Boudhanath Stupa suffered greatly during the 2015 earthquake of Nepal, but was eventually preserved
back to its normal state.





Kathmandu Durbar Square

Kathmandu Durbar square is one of three durbar squares in Kathmandu.

There are 3 durbar squares in Kathmandu as it was once home to 3 individual kingdoms(Kathmandu, Lalitpur
and Bhaktapur) before being united as one country.Kathmandu Durbar square acted as a royal palace for many
ruling families in Kathmandu until the 19th century, most notably the Shah and Malla dynasty.

The area consists of various pagoda-style temples with extremely detailed designs. Many of these structures
date back to the 3rd century. Now, the temples and structures are treated as open areas for the general public.

Kathmandu Durbar square was also enlisted as a Unesco World Heritage site in 1979.





Janaki Mandir

Janaki Mandir is a hindu temple located in Janakpur, in the terai region of Nepal.

It is one of the renowned Hindu temples, dedicated to Hindu Goddess Sita, who marries Lord Ram, who played a
significant role in the Ramayana. Every year, thousands of pilgrims from all over the country and India visit the site
to worship the Sita statue in the inner sanctum. Increasing numbers of visitors occur during auspicious times like
Ram Nawami, Dashain and Tihar.

The temple was constructed with inspiration from Mughal and Hindu architecture. The temple dates back to 1910.It
is a three-storied structure and made entirely of stone and marble with 60 rooms.
